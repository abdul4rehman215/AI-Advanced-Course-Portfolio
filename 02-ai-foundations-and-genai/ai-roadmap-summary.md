# AI Roadmap Summary

The first part of the course built a broad picture of what Artificial Intelligence actually means. Instead of starting immediately with tools or code, it began by framing AI as a large field with multiple layers inside it.

The clearest structure I learned here is that Artificial Intelligence is the overall umbrella, Machine Learning is a subset of AI, and Deep Learning is a subset of machine learning. This hierarchy is important because many people use these terms interchangeably, but they are not the same thing. Understanding the hierarchy made the course easier to follow.

I also learned that AI is not limited to one profession. It can be applied to business, security, healthcare, retail, logistics, education, and many other areas. That idea matters because it changes AI from “something to study” into “something to apply.”

Another key takeaway was the distinction between Artificial Narrow Intelligence and Artificial General Intelligence. Most systems we use today fall into the narrow category because they are designed for specific tasks. AGI is still more of a future-oriented idea. This helped me stay grounded and understand that practical AI work today is mostly focused on specialized systems.

The roadmap also introduced the idea that different AI systems depend on different forms of data, different model architectures, and different goals. Some systems classify. Some generate. Some learn patterns from labeled data. Others work with unlabeled data or with reward-based learning.

My main takeaway from this note is that AI should be understood as a connected landscape:
- a broad field
- multiple learning approaches
- multiple data forms
- multiple real-world applications

That perspective became the starting point for everything else in this section.

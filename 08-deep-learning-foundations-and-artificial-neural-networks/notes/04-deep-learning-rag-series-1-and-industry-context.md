# Deep Learning, RAG Series 1, and Industry Context

This note captures the lecture that acted like a bridge between deep learning foundations and later advanced AI topics.

The lecture was different from a purely technical ANN lecture because it also connected the material to real-world AI trends. It positioned deep learning as part of a larger ecosystem that includes NLP, generative AI, and Retrieval-Augmented Generation.

A useful concept from this session was the explanation that deep learning is a sub-domain of machine learning, but one that becomes especially important when the data is unstructured. That includes images, audio, and language. This helped connect the earlier deep learning lectures with the later sections on transformers and RAG.

The lecture also highlighted the role of neurons and activation functions in a simplified way. ReLU was presented as especially important in deep learning practice, particularly for computer vision.

Another important part was the distinction between discriminative and generative AI. That distinction helped me see how classification models and generation-focused models relate to one another rather than thinking of them as totally separate worlds.

The session also introduced RAG at a high level. I liked that because it planted the idea early: retrieval, augmentation, and generation can be combined to produce grounded answers from external knowledge. That made the later RAG block easier to anticipate.

My takeaway from this lecture is that the course was not teaching isolated topics. It was building a connected map from ML to deep learning to modern AI systems.

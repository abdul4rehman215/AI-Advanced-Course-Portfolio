# What Is Data Science

Data science was introduced in the course as a multidisciplinary field that combines statistics, computing, and domain understanding in order to extract useful insights from data.

What stood out to me is that data science is not only about building models. It is a full process that begins with data collection and understanding, continues through preparation and analysis, and ends with reporting and decision support.

This means data science includes:
- asking the right questions
- understanding the context of the data
- cleaning and exploring data
- preparing it for analysis or modeling
- turning results into useful communication

Another useful takeaway is that data itself is often described as a valuable asset. Businesses, platforms, and organizations generate large amounts of data, but raw data alone is not useful. Its value appears only when it is understood and processed properly.

The course also reinforced the importance of domain knowledge. A strong technical workflow is helpful, but understanding the business or domain meaning of the data makes the analysis more accurate and more useful.

Overall, this note helped me see data science as a process-driven discipline rather than a single toolset.

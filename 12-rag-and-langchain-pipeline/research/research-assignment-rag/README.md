# Research Assignment — Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)

This folder contains the RAG research assignment completed for the course.

## Research focus
This research work is centered on understanding Retrieval-Augmented Generation as an evidence-grounding layer for AI systems, along with its current and potential use cases across industries.

## Main areas covered
- what RAG is and why it matters
- how RAG combines retrieval with generation
- why RAG is useful for private, changing, and domain-specific knowledge
- existing enterprise use cases for grounded assistants
- document question answering and support workflows
- future opportunities such as agentic retrieval and multimodal retrieval
- strengths, limitations, and adoption tradeoffs
- use-case analysis across industries in a structured research format
